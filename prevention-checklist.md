### **Staying safe in a digital-first campus environment requires more than just a strong password. As a student in India, you are navigating a landscape of high-speed UPI transactions and shared hostel networks, which come with specific risks.**



Here is an actionable **"Stay Safe Online"** checklist designed for your daily digital life.



#### **The Student's Digital Safety Checklist**



1. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Use an authenticator app (like Google Authenticator) for your Gmail, LinkedIn, and Instagram. Avoid relying solely on SMS, which can be intercepted via SIM swapping.



2\. **The UPI "Golden Rule":** Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Whether it’s a "refund" or a "scholarship," a PIN is only required to send or debit money from your account.



3\. **Verify Before You Scan:** When using QR codes in public places or through WhatsApp, double-check the "Payee Name" that appears on your screen before hitting 'Proceed.' Scammers often overlay fake QR codes on top of legitimate ones.



4\. **Use a Secure Connection (VPN):** If you are using the hostel Wi-Fi or a cafe’s open network to access your bank or university portal, use a VPN to encrypt your data and prevent "Man-in-the-Middle" attacks.



5\. **Audit App Permissions:** Periodically check which apps have access to your Contacts, Location, and Microphone. Many "free" utility apps are actually data-harvesting tools.



6\. **Secure Your Physical Device:** Set a strong biometric lock (FaceID/Fingerprint) and a 6-digit PIN on your phone. If your device is stolen in a crowded area, this is your first line of defense for your linked bank accounts.



7\. **Identify "Urgency" as a Red Flag:** Be skeptical of any message (Email, SMS, or WhatsApp) that creates a sense of panic—such as "Your electricity will be cut" or "Your account is blocked"—and asks you to click a link.



8\. **Practice "Digital Hygiene" for Credentials:** Never save your passwords in a plain passwords.txt file or in your browser's "Auto-fill" on shared library computers. Use a dedicated Password Manager instead.



#### **How to Report a Cybercrime**



If you or a batchmate fall victim to a scam, **time is the most critical factor**, especially in financial fraud where the "Golden Hour" (the first 2 hours) is your best chance to freeze the stolen funds.



1. **National Helpline:** Call 1930 immediately to report financial fraud.



2\. **Web Portal:** File a formal complaint at the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: cybercrime.gov.in.



3\. **Local Action:** Keep screenshots of the transaction ID, the attacker's phone number, and the chat history as evidence for the police.
