### **For a college student, your social media presence is essentially a digital extension of your resume. Balancing your personal life with professional aspirations requires a strategic approach to what you post and how you interact.**



#### **Here is a checklist of Do's and Don'ts for responsible social media use:**



##### **Social Media Responsibility Checklist**





|                                 **Do's (Best Practices)**|                                     **Don'ts (Avoid These)**|
|-|-|
|**Audit Your Privacy Settings:** Regularly check who can see your posts and tagged photos.|**Post During Class:** Avoid posting or "checking in" when you are supposed to be in a lecture or lab.|
|**Use Professional Headshots:** Use high-quality, edited photos for platforms like LinkedIn and GitHub.|**Engage in Online Arguments:** Avoid "flame wars" or disrespectful comments on public forums.|
|**Fact-Check Before Sharing:** Verify news or academic information before reposting to avoid spreading misinformation.|**Share Sensitive Data:** Never post photos of your ID cards, hostel room numbers, or hall tickets.|
|**Network with Intent:** Follow industry leaders, alumni, and companies related to AI and ML.|**Complain About Faculty/College:** Keep grievances private; digital venting can have long-term academic consequences.|
|**Showcase Your Projects:** Share updates on your hackathon progress, GitHub links, or Kaggle achievements.|**Post Illegal or Risky Content:** Avoid any content that violates university policy or local laws.|
