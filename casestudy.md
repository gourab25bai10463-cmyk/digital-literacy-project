### **Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud (The "Refund" Scam)**



**UPI (Unified Payments Interface) Fraud** is a deceptive cybercrime where attackers trick victims into authorizing unauthorized fund transfers from their linked bank accounts. Unlike technical hacks, this relies on **social engineering**—manipulating the victim's trust and lack of technical familiarity with payment apps.



#### **How It Happens: Step-by-Step**



1. **The Hook:** The attacker calls the victim, posing as a customer service executive from a popular e-commerce site or a bank. They claim there is a "pending refund" or a "technical glitch" with a recent transaction.



2\. **The Instruction:** The victim is asked to open their UPI app (like GPay or PhonePe) and "scan a QR code" or click a "Request" notification sent by the attacker to "verify" their account.



3\. **The Trap:** The attacker sends a **"Collect Request"** instead of a payment. They tell the victim to enter their UPI PIN to "receive" the money.



4\. **The Execution:** Once the victim enters the PIN, the money is instantly debited from their account and moved to the attacker’s account, often through a series of "mule" accounts to hide the trail.



#### **Target and Consequences**

The primary targets are often **university students** selling items on second-hand marketplaces or **elderly individuals** who may not realize that a UPI PIN is only required for sending money, never for receiving it.



The consequences go beyond the immediate **financial loss**. For a student, losing a semester's worth of hostel fees or allowance can lead to severe **psychological stress** and academic disruption. Legally, the recovery of funds is difficult once the transaction is authorized by a PIN, making prevention and digital literacy the only effective shields.



#### **Pro-Tip for Safety**

Always remember the golden rule of UPI: **You only enter your PIN to PAY money, never to RECEIVE it**. If someone asks for your PIN to send you a refund, it is a 100% certainty that it is a scam.
